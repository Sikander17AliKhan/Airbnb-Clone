Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to common questions

To book a stay, browse listings on our platform, select your desired property, choose your check-in and check-out dates, specify the number of guests, and click "Reserve." You'll be guided through the payment process. Once confirmed, you'll receive a booking confirmation email.
Cancellation policies vary by listing — each host sets their own policy (Flexible, Moderate, or Strict). You can find the cancellation policy on any listing page before you book. Generally, flexible policies offer full refunds if cancelled 24+ hours before check-in.
Click "Become a host" at the top of the page. You'll be guided through listing your property — describe your space, add photos, set your price, and define your availability. Your listing goes live after review. Airbnb charges a small host service fee on each booking.
AirCover is our comprehensive protection for both guests and hosts. For guests, it includes booking protection, check-in guarantee, 24-hour safety line, and listing accuracy guarantee. For hosts, it includes up to $3 million in damage protection and $1 million liability insurance — all at no additional cost.
Once you have a confirmed booking, you can message your host directly through Airbnb's messaging system. Go to your trip details and click "Message host." All communication is kept on platform for safety and record-keeping purposes.
Airbnb accepts credit/debit cards, UPI, net banking, and various other payment methods. Payment is collected when you book. Hosts receive payment 24 hours after guest check-in. All transactions are secured with industry-standard encryption.
